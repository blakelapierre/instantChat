End-to-End encryption over Peer-to-Peer channels entirely from your browser without any "apps" or "plugins" is not only possible, but ACTUALLY EXISTS, right now.

If your browser supports WebRTC (Chrome, Firefox, or Android) you can, right now, share any file you want, audio, video, text with anyone in the world without ANYONE else being able to see or know ANYTHING about that file.

We can bust through most NATs and firewalls, to deliver true, peer-to-peer, fully end-to-end encrypted communications from an extremely simple and easy to use interface.

This is Alpha-quality, and runs on Firefox, Chrome, and Android browsers. They are the only ones that have completed enough of the WebRTC spec.
